Photo Requirements

What should I wear for passport photos?

We recommend wearing solid-colored clothing in a color that contrasts with the white background. Avoid white or very light clothing, as well as patterns or logos. For most passport photos, you should not wear uniforms, hats, or head coverings (unless worn for religious purposes).

Can I wear glasses in my passport photo?

For most passport photos, including Canadian, U.S., and many other countries, glasses are no longer permitted. If you must wear glasses for medical reasons, you'll need to provide a signed note from your doctor or medical professional.

Can I smile in my passport photo?

Requirements vary by country. For Canadian passport photos, a neutral expression with closed mouth is required. For U.S. passport photos, a natural smile is acceptable. Our photographers know the specific requirements for each country and will guide you accordingly.

Can I wear jewelry or accessories?

Small, non-distracting jewelry is generally acceptable. However, avoid large earrings or facial piercings that cast shadows or obscure facial features. Hair should be kept away from the face, and headbands or hair accessories should not be visible.

Special Circumstances

Can you take passport photos for babies and children?

Yes, we specialize in taking passport photos for babies and children of all ages. Our photographers are patient and experienced with young children. For babies, we have special techniques to ensure we capture compliant photos while keeping them comfortable.

Do you guarantee acceptance of your passport photos?

Yes, we guarantee that your photos will be accepted by the relevant government agency. If your photos are rejected for any technical reason, we'll retake them for free.

Can I get digital copies of my passport photos?

Yes, we offer digital copies of your passport photos for online applications. These can be emailed to you immediately after your session.

Can you help with photos for religious or medical head coverings?

Yes, we are experienced in taking passport photos for individuals who wear religious head coverings. Most countries allow head coverings for religious reasons, but your full facial features must still be visible from the bottom of your chin to the top of your forehead, and from ear to ear.
