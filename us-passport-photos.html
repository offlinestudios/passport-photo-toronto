U.S. Passport Photo Requirements

The U.S. Department of State has specific requirements for passport photos that differ from Canadian standards. Our service ensures your photos will comply with all these requirements:

  • 2x2 inches (51x51 mm) in size
  • Taken within the last 6 months
  • Full face view directly facing the camera
  • Neutral facial expression or natural smile
  • Both eyes open and clearly visible
  • Plain white or off-white background
  • No glasses (as of 2016 U.S. regulations)
  • No hats or head coverings (except for religious purposes)
  • Normal clothing (no uniforms or similar attire)
  • Proper lighting with no shadows on face or background

Common U.S. Passport Photo Mistakes to Avoid

Many passport applications are delayed due to photo issues. Here are common mistakes we help you avoid:

  • Incorrect size (must be exactly 2x2 inches)
  • Wrong background color (must be white or off-white)
  • Shadows on face or background
  • Wearing glasses (not permitted since 2016)
  • Smiling too broadly (neutral expression preferred)
  • Head tilted or not facing directly forward
  • Poor lighting or image quality
  • Photos older than 6 months

U.S. Passport vs. Canadian Passport Photos

It's important to understand that U.S. and Canadian passport photos have different requirements:

  • Size: U.S. photos are 2x2 inches, Canadian photos are 50mm x 70mm
  • Background: Both require white backgrounds, but U.S. specs are more strict
  • Glasses: U.S. photos don't allow glasses, Canadian photos may allow them in some cases
  • Expression: U.S. allows neutral expression or natural smile, Canadian requires neutral
  • Head size: Different proportional requirements for head size within the frame

Our photographers are trained in both sets of requirements and will ensure your photos meet the specific standards for your intended use.
