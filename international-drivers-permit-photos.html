About International Driver's Permits

An International Driver's Permit (IDP) is a document that translates your driver's license information into multiple languages, allowing you to drive legally in foreign countries. Here's some general information about IDPs:

  • An IDP is valid for one year from the date of issue
  • You must carry both your IDP and your original driver's license when driving abroad
  • In Canada, IDPs are issued by the Canadian Automobile Association (CAA)
  • You must be at least 18 years old and have a valid Canadian driver's license to apply
  • The IDP is recognized in over 150 countries worldwide
  • One or two photos are typically required for the application

Countries That Require an IDP

Many countries require or recommend an International Driver's Permit for foreign drivers, including:

  • Most European countries (including Italy, Spain, Germany, and Greece)
  • Japan
  • South Korea
  • Thailand
  • Brazil
  • China
  • India
  • Russia
  • United Arab Emirates
  • Many African and South American countries

Even in countries where an IDP is not legally required, it is often recommended as it provides a universally recognized form of identification and helps overcome language barriers.

IDP Application Information

In Canada, you can apply for an International Driver's Permit at any CAA office. Here's what you'll need:

  • A valid Canadian driver's license
  • Two identical passport-type photos
  • A completed application form
  • The application fee (approximately $25-30)

For the most current information about International Driver's Permit applications, please visit the official CAA website.
